A Rugby League Results and Latest scores App.

Rugby League Results and updates App - The Project Description

Many people enjoy watching Rugby League. We understand that Rugby League fans have busy and full employment and social lives.

I was asked to conduct a study into how fans of Rugby League would like to be kept abreast of the latest scores and news from Rugby League Super League

Rugby League App - Paper prototype

The project design began with paper prototypes of the project.

Rugby League App - Persona's & User Journey Mapping

Three Persona's & User Journey's were created to ensure empathy to a broad demographic of end users was maintained.

Persona's & User Journey Mapping - Callum Heap, schoolboy, age 12 years

Callum represents the youth supporter. He is a school boy who attend the live matches with his father.

When Callum's father is working, his is not able to attend the live matches.

Callum would like to be kept up to date of Rugby League scores and news on these accasions

Persona's & User Journey Mapping - Jack O'Brien, student, age 20 years

Jack represents the younger adult demographic. Jack is living away from home at University.

Jack's University home is too far away for him to attend the live games.

Jack would like to be kept up to date of Rugby League scores and news whilst he is living away at University.

Persona's & User Journey Mapping - Jean Fletcher, retired primary school administator, age 61 years

Jack represents the senior adult demographic. Jean is enjoying a full retired life.

Jean enjoys walking with her local ramblers group. The group typically walk on a Sunday, which is often Matchday in Rugby League, and so, Jean is not able to attend the live games .

Jean would like to be kept up to date of Rugby League scores and news whilst he is enjoying a display walking with her rambblers group.

User Testing of Rugby League App - remote user testing - due to COVID-19 pandemic.

Because of the COVID-19 outbreak of Spring/Summer 2020, the user testing was conducted remotely with Neil and recorded on his smart phone.

Neil used AZ screen recorder for his user testing, which was his choice. Sadly AZ screen recorder affected the responsivity of the buttons and appeared initially to indicate that buttons were not working or that the user could not press them. This was not the case. The user tester tried the prototype again without the AZ screen recorder running and reported all buttons worked as expected.

Four key reflections from the user tester observations.

The user tester appeared to perceive that clicking on the club badges accomplishes a function, that club badges direct him to information related to that club. At 2min & 22sec and 5min & 31sec he states: “So, the links at the top? The club badges?” & “If I click on this Catalans logo at the top? Nothing seems to be happening!”. In keeping with Don Norman’s real or perceived affordances, these graphics should therefore provide a link, be redesigned or be removed.

On changing colour settings, the user tester commented, “Not sure”, at 2min & 56sec. On asking for clarification from the tester his response was that he wasn’t sure which type of colour blindness he has, so didn’t know which selection to make. This is something the observer could not anticipate in the design process.

Where the user appears to be figuring out how the app works, he states, “Ah! So, I click on the game & it’s brought it up”, at 3min & 4sec. This response suggests that this element of the app design is in keeping with Krugs recommendation ‘UX design should be easy enough to understand that a user who has never seen the application before can look at it and think “Oh, it’s a _____”.’.

This could be explained as an AZ screen recorder processing issue or the back button may be too small, contravening Fitts’ Law.

Rugby League App - Key Takeaways
Prototype alterations to improve usability

Key takeaways as a result of user testing

As a result of this analysis my recommendations for improvement include, the inclusion of a ‘Help’ option which the user tester stated would be beneficial. This recommendation is in line with Nielsen’s heuristic that help and documentation are necessary. Also, the size of the back button may need to be increased.

Reassuringly, the user tester was able to quickly learn how to navigate the app, indicating it has been designed effectively with empathy for the end user ensuring that a person of average ability or experience is able to use it for its intended purpose, without frustration.
